# NNNN. {Short title of the decision — a declarative statement, not a question}

Date: {YYYY-MM-DD}
Status: {Proposed | Accepted | Deprecated | Superseded by ADR-NNNN}

## Context

What forces are in play? State the problem, the constraints (including ones not
visible in the code — compliance, latency budgets, partner contracts), and what
prompted the decision now. Name the genuine alternatives that existed. Two to
four sentences is usually enough; cite the source that triggered this if one
exists (a tracker unit matching $ISSUE_ID_PATTERN, a prior ADR, a design doc).

## Decision

What we are doing, in the active voice and the present tense: "We do X." Be
concrete and specific enough that a reader can tell whether a future change
contradicts it. If this decision overrides an earlier one, say which ADR it
supersedes.

## Rejected alternatives

The options we considered and turned down, each with the specific reason — so
nobody re-proposes them in six months. Record a rejection only when it is
non-obvious; the explicit no-s are as valuable as the yes. Omit this section
entirely if there were no real alternatives worth remembering.

- **{Alternative A}** — rejected because {specific reason}.
- **{Alternative B}** — rejected because {specific reason}.

## Consequences

The non-obvious downstream effects — what becomes easier, what becomes harder,
and the limitations we are knowingly accepting. Include a **revisit trigger**:
the concrete signal that would make us reopen this decision. Omit this section if
the effects are obvious.

- {Positive effect — what this buys us.}
- {Accepted limitation — what we are trading away on purpose.}
- **Revisit trigger:** {the concrete condition under which we reconsider.}
